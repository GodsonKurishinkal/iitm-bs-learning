## Recommended Approach

### Option 1: Incremental Learning-Driven Creation ‚≠ê RECOMMENDED

**Strategy**: Create content as you actually study each course

**Advantages**:
- Content based on real learning experience
- Authentic insights and challenges
- No wasted effort on courses you haven't taken
- Better retention (learning by teaching)
- Natural pace (2-3 weeks per course)

**Process**:
1. Watch IIT Madras lectures for Week X
2. Take rough notes while learning
3. Polish notes using the Week 1 template
4. Create Jupyter notebook with examples you tried
5. Add your own insights and stumbling blocks
6. Move to next week

**Timeline**: 
- Per course: 12 weeks (at 1 week/week pace)
- Foundation level: 6-8 months naturally

### Option 2: AI-Assisted Batch Generation

**Strategy**: Use AI (Claude/GPT) to generate content based on lecture transcripts

**Process**:
1. Get transcripts/slides from IIT Madras videos
2. Feed to AI with Week 1 as template
3. Generate comprehensive notes
4. Human review and enhance
5. Create notebooks with code examples

**Advantages**:
- Fast (can complete in 2-3 weeks)
- Consistent quality
- Comprehensive coverage

**Challenges**:
- Needs access to lecture content
- Requires significant review time
- May lack personal insights
- Quality depends on source material

### Option 3: Hybrid Approach ‚ö° BEST OF BOTH

**Strategy**: AI generates first draft, you enhance while studying

**Process**:
1. Use AI to create 80% draft from syllabus
2. Study the course week by week
3. Enhance draft with your understanding
4. Add real examples you worked through
5. Fill gaps and fix errors

**Timeline**: 
- Upfront: 1-2 weeks for all drafts
- Enhancement: As you study (natural pace)
